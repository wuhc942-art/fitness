# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Chinese fitness users who want a practical mobile tool for recording, repeating, and reviewing workouts. They may train at a gym or at home, often reuse chest, back, leg, or full-body routines, and need fast access to previous weights, reps, templates, plans, body data, diet records, and progress.

The owner is building the app personally and prefers clear non-technical handoff, small verified improvements, and release-safe changes.

## Product Purpose

FitAI is a lightweight WeChat mini program for personal fitness tracking. It helps users quickly record daily training, reuse common templates, plan training dates, review history and body data, and make incremental progress without turning the app into a heavy coaching platform.

Success means the app feels trustworthy and smooth on mobile, preserves existing workout, template, plan, history, body, diet, reminder, and profile flows, remains buildable for WeChat, and keeps the upload package comfortably below the main package limit.

## Brand Personality

Clean, focused, encouraging.

The product should feel closer to Apple-style mobile software: calm, direct, polished, and useful. It should give users confidence that their records are safe and easy to continue, while avoiding clutter, noisy color, and decorative complexity.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI-purple gradient dashboards, dark-tech hero screens, marketing landing-page composition, oversized decorative cards, flashy animations, and fitness apps that feel like social media feeds.

Avoid heavy media assets in the main package, dense text-heavy screens, inconsistent button styles, unclear empty states, and UI that looks like a demo rather than a daily-use tool.

## Design Principles

1. Start from the user's next workout. The home screen and primary actions should help the user decide what to do next in seconds.
2. Make repeated training effortless. Plans, templates, history repeat, and previous performance should feel connected.
3. Use restraint as polish. Hierarchy, spacing, typography, and one consistent accent should carry the interface more than decoration.
4. Protect user records. Save, edit, delete, loading, empty, and error states should be explicit and reassuring.
5. Stay release-safe. Prefer small improvements, avoid large assets, preserve existing data flows, and verify every meaningful change with a WeChat build.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use readable Chinese typography, strong contrast, clear touch targets, and plain labels. Motion should be subtle and state-driven, with reduced-motion-friendly behavior. Color must not be the only way to communicate status, and empty/error states should explain what the user can do next.
